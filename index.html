A personal essay

The Open
Highway

On motion, silence, and the road that asks nothing of you

01

The Departure

I don't really know how to explain the highway thing without it coming out wrong. You just get on and something loosens up. I left because I couldn't be in the house anymore. Wasn't going anywhere specific. Just needed to go.

02

The Land

Flat out there where I was. Grass, fence posts, some trees so far off they looked fake. A grain elevator I pass every time and never think about. Hawks always hawks on that stretch, I swear. The sky got huge at some point and I stopped noticing the road for a second. Too much going on up there.

03

The Golden Hour

This old song came on. One I used to wear out completely. I turned it up way too loud and just sang, didn't have to think about the words at all, they were already there. Light was going golden by then, that late afternoon thing where everything looks a little better than it is. I just kept thinking okay, okay not about anything, just that.

04

The Weight

There's a call I've been not making for two weeks. Some other stuff too, nothing that would sound like much if I said it out loud. It all just sat quieter out there. I don't think anything actually changed. I just got away from it far enough to stop feeling it right on top of me.

05

The Return

Turned around when it got dark. Came home. Felt more like myself, mostly. I know that sounds like nothing but it wasn't nothing.

"The road asked nothing of me except to keep going, and for a while, that was enough."
